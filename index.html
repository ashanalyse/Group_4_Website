Introduction to Information Technology

The "Introduction to Information Technology" course at GIS offers a comprehensive foundation in IT, preparing students for the digital age. Covering topics like computer fundamentals, software applications, networking, web development, cybersecurity, and basic programming, this course provides a well-rounded understanding of IT essentials. Through hands-on projects and practical assignments, students apply their knowledge, creating personal websites, setting up networks, and developing basic software applications. Assessments include quizzes, demonstrations, projects, and occasional exams to evaluate both theoretical comprehension and practical application. Graduates gain digital literacy, setting them on a path toward advanced IT studies or careers in fields like computer science, software development, network administration, and cybersecurity, with opportunities across diverse industries reliant on technology.

Introduction to Mathematics

The "Introduction to Mathematics" course provides a comprehensive foundation in essential mathematical concepts, problem-solving techniques, and critical thinking skills. Tailored to accommodate varying levels of prior knowledge, the curriculum covers areas such as arithmetic, algebra, geometry, and basic statistics. Introduction to Political Science

The "Introduction to Political Science" course provides students with a comprehensive foundation in the study of politics, governance, and international relations. Through a combination of lectures, discussions, readings, and projects, students will gain an understanding of various political systems, ideologies, and theories. The course covers topics such as policy analysis, comparative politics, and international relations, offering a global perspective on political phenomena. Students will also develop research skills and engage in independent research projects. Assessment methods include examinations, research projects, class participation, and essays. The course is open to all students interested in political science, regardless of prior coursework in the subject. Overall, it aims to equip students with critical thinking skills and a deep insight into the complexities of the political world.
